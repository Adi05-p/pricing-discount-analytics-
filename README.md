# Pricing & Discount Analytics for Fashion E-Commerce

## Overview
This project analyses the impact of pricing strategies, discount levels, and seasonal sales on customer purchase behaviour in the fashion e-commerce industry.

The objective is to identify how pricing decisions influence purchase frequency and to translate analytical insights into actionable managerial recommendations.

---

## Dataset
- Synthetic dataset with 5,000 customer records
- Variables include:
  - Base price
  - Discount percentage
  - Seasonal period
  - Promotion exposure
  - Purchase frequency
  - Average order value

The dataset was designed to reflect realistic fashion e-commerce behaviour.

---

## Tools & Technologies
- Python (Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn, Statsmodels)
- Power BI
- Kaggle Notebook Environment

---

## Analytical Approach
- Descriptive statistics to understand pricing and discount distributions
- Correlation analysis to identify relationships between variables
- Regression analysis to quantify the impact of pricing factors on purchase frequency
- Aggregation into price bands for managerial interpretation
- Interactive Power BI dashboard for insight communication

---

## Key Insights
- Moderate discounts significantly increase purchase frequency
- Higher base prices negatively impact purchase behaviour
- Seasonal sales amplify customer purchasing activity
- Excessive discounts show diminishing returns

---

## Managerial Implications
- Focus on moderate discounting rather than extreme markdowns
- Leverage seasonal sales strategically for revenue uplift
- Adopt data-driven pricing strategies instead of blanket discounts
